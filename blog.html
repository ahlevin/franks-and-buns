How to Get a High Score in Franks & Buns

Getting a great score in Franks & Buns isn't just about tapping fast - it's about knowing when to fly high, when to drop, and how to chain multipliers together. Here's everything you need to know to dominate the leaderboard.

Master the tap rhythm

The most important skill in Franks & Buns is developing a consistent tap rhythm. Unlike random mashing, a steady rhythm lets you predict exactly where your frank will be when a bun passes. Think of it like a heartbeat - tap, tap, release, drop. The game rewards precision over speed.

Always chase the condiment buns

Regular buns score 100 points. A jalapeño bun scores 1000. That's a 10x difference on a single landing. Condiment buns are marked with a glowing squeeze bottle - mustard for 2x, ketchup for 3x, relish and cheese for 5x, and the rare jalapeño for 10x. Always prioritize them even if it means a tricky flight path.

Build your streak above everything else

Missing a bun resets your streak to zero. At 3 in a row you get a 1.5x multiplier. At 5 in a row it's 2x. At 10 in a row - Wiener Wizard mode - every landing is worth 3x points. A jalapeño bun during a Wiener Wizard streak is worth 3000 points in a single drop. Protecting your streak is more valuable than chasing a difficult bun.

Fill boxes fast for the speed bonus

Every 6 franks fills a box and awards a bonus. Boxes completed multiply in value - box 1 gives 100 points, box 5 gives 500, box 10 gives 1000. The speed increase after each box makes the game harder but also means more buns scroll past per minute, giving you more scoring opportunities if you can keep up.

The dog is always worth it

After your third box, a dog starts appearing on screen. It's not a threat - flying your frank through it earns an instant 1000 point bonus with no risk. Always go for the dog. It's free points.

Dodge the fork early, risk it later

In the early game, always dodge the chef's fork - losing a life at low score breaks your streak and resets momentum. But late in a run with a big streak going, sometimes it's worth gambling on a tight pass. The fork has a brief warning before it fully extends, giving you a fraction of a second to react.

What's New in Franks & Buns

Since launching Franks & Buns we've been steadily adding features and polishing the game based on how it plays in the real world. Here's a rundown of what's been added.

The opening cutscene

The game now opens with a short animated cutscene that sets the scene - a dog steals a hot dog, the chef gives chase with fork raised, the dog trips, and the frank goes airborne. That flying frank is you. The whole sequence takes about two seconds and can be skipped with a tap if you want to get straight into it.

The chef's arm obstacle

Partway through the game a chef's arm rises from the bottom of the screen holding a fork. Hit the tines and you lose a life. The arm now uses a fully illustrated image of a chef's hand gripping a silver fork, complete with a white chef's sleeve and cuff detail. A brief warning flash appears before it arrives so it never feels completely unfair.

Gameplay dogs

After filling your third box, dogs start appearing as you play. Fly your frank through one for an instant 1000 point bonus. They float through the kitchen at random heights with a pulsing gold ring around them so you can spot them easily.

Hot dog rolls

The buns were redesigned to look like proper elongated hot dog rolls rather than round burger buns. They now feature sesame seeds on top, a visible slit, and a toasted gradient that looks genuinely appetising.

Condiment squeeze bottles

Condiment bonuses are now shown as drawn squeeze bottles - yellow for mustard, red for ketchup, green for relish, orange for cheese, and deep red for jalapeño. Each bottle has a gradient body, nozzle, cap, and a small drip at the tip so they're immediately recognisable at a glance.

Mute button

A mute/unmute button now lives in the bottom left corner of the game screen. Tap it to silence all sounds without changing your device volume. It defaults to unmuted and stays visible on every screen.
